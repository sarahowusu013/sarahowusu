These posters were inspired by the book 'Afrikan Alphabets: The Story of Writing in Afrika' which Saki Mafundikwa wrote. In this book, Mafundikwa shows the different African writing systems hidden away from use and not mentioned in the graphic design history books. Because of this, he encourages people to use their African heritage to create something culturally relevant. I was always taught the same designers and never about black art growing up. so, I never really knew or saw designers like me. So after hearing about Mafundikwa and all the African alphabets he’s discovered, I've decided to create posters using different African writing systems to show the missing history of typography and let people know that this is happening. By doing this, I used letters that looked recognizable to write a message but kept the original saying of the letter to show that it's not English. I’ve Incorporated Ankara Prints to make it clear to the viewers that this is about the African culture as their design of colours and patterns are well known.
